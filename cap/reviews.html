First Time Props Coordinator

The props coordinator plays an essential role in a theatrical production. A prop is anything an actor interacts with on stage. This can include something as small as a pen or a larger item like a sword or shield. What is it like to be the person in charge of bringing the props to life from a script?

I was props coordinator for an original play called “Wedlocked.” Overall, I had a very positive experience working on this show. If you enjoy working with your hands or organizational tasks, this could be a role you would enjoy.

I found the process of getting into the role to be very straightforward, even for a newcomer. First, I filled out a form expressing my interest and previous experience. Then I was contacted to do an interview with the director, where I explained how I'd contribute to the show and what my vision was. After being accepted, I took a closer look at the script and started to make a spreadsheet of what I needed to make or buy.

Going into the process, I wish I had a little more guidance for building items that would be used on stage, as some of my props were not very durable and needed to be handled in a specific way. My biggest task for the show was figuring out what I wanted to do for a scene where the hotel the show was set in burned down. My solution was to try cardboard fires like the one pictured above. During the show two run crew members stood in front of the stage holding the fire while the actors threw fake buckets of water at them. While this solution worked there are other methods I could have used to achieve having a fire on stage that looked better than floating cardboard.

There was also another scene where the characters are walking on the beach so I made cardboard seaweed to put in the background. While it initially looked good as the rehearsal process went on the seaweed started to fall apart and it became clear that the seaweed was not made sturdy enough.

There were other props I do think came out well and stood the test of time. The largest one that stands out was transitioning the dining room to make it look like it burned down in the fire. I brought a second table cloth that I cut up and added burn marks to and I brought a second set of cups and plates and broke some of them and added burn marks. There was an issue with how many pieces this included though, While it looked good on stage it did take too long to set up between scenes.

It is important for a props coordinator to think about organizing what is in the show but also making the props durable and safe for actors and run crew to use. I enjoyed the challenge of having to figure out how to achieve all those things. If you also enjoy a creative challenge, being the props coordinator for a show might be for you!

Is a sketch show for you?

When most people think of stage acting they think of a play or a musical but there are other types of roles you can take on. This past Halloween I acted in a sketch show called “The Fall of Scott Hall.” For those not familiar with what a sketch show is, think of the style of a show like SNL. It is a show consisting of multiple short comedic skits. For someone new to acting this might be less intimidating then jumping into a full play.

For the process of getting into this show, I attended auditions and was asked to read for two scenes. The audition process was very straightforward and did not take long. Then a few days later I received my acceptance. For this show I was in nine sketches with roles of varying size. In some sketches I was a main character and in others I only appeared onstage for one or two lines.

The show had three directors who each directed four sketches so I worked under the direction of multiple people. I enjoyed the variety in directing styles but this could be overwhelming for someone new. If you are the type of person who prefers to play one character and study them deeply this would not be the style of show for you.

My biggest struggle in this show would be that comedy is not the style of acting I'm best at. However if you are like me you may still enjoy acting in a sketch show. A show can’t only have comedic roles. There is also a need for a "Straight man” to react to how absurd the other characters are.

While preparing I went to rehearsal three to five times a week and practiced each scene three times before tech week. Tech week is the week before the show where the actors rehearse with souls, lights, costumes and props. This is the longest most time consuming part of the process as it usually involves many long and late nights. This was my favorite part of the process because I got to see everything coming together for the first time.If you are looking for a casual commitment then I would be hesitant to act in a show. It's important to make sure you are fully aware of the time commitment before deciding to sign up.

If you enjoy comedy or want to dip your toes into acting, being in a sketch show might be perfect for you. I really enjoyed being in this show as it felt different from other things I had acted in before and I enjoyed the change. It gave me the change to work on things I don't typically get to do in a show.
